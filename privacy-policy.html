Privacy Policy

We take privacy as seriously as performance.

This policy explains how Fidelis Agency collects, stores, and protects the data you share with us online or during engagements. It aligns with GDPR, Australian Privacy Principles, and other global regulations relevant to our clients.

Effective date: January 17, 2025 Last reviewed: January 17, 2025

1. Information we collect

We only request information that helps us understand your project and deliver our services.

  • Contact details such as your name, company, email, and phone number.
  • Project notes, budgets, and goals shared during discovery calls or intake forms.
  • Analytics data (cookies, device data, IP address) that tells us how visitors use our website.
  • Payment and invoicing details processed through trusted financial providers.

2. How we use your data

Every data point is tied to a clear business purpose.

  • Preparing proposals, quotes, and service agreements tailored to your needs.
  • Delivering contracted design, development, and marketing services.
  • Sending operational updates or marketing emails (you can opt out anytime).
  • Improving our website experience through aggregated, anonymised analytics.

3. Legal bases for processing

The legal basis depends on the context of the data collection:

  • Contractual necessity for clients who have engaged Fidelis Agency.
  • Legitimate interest when we respond to enquiries from prospective clients.
  • Consent for marketing communications, cookies, or testimonials.
  • Legal obligation where billing or tax records must be preserved.

4. Data storage, retention & security

We use reputable cloud providers with encryption at rest and in transit. Internal access is role-based and audited.

  • Client files are retained for as long as we collaborate plus five years for compliance.
  • Prospect enquiries are removed after 18 months of inactivity.
  • You may request deletion sooner unless legal obligations require retention.

5. Third parties & international transfers

We only share data with partners essential to service delivery:

  • Hosting, analytics, communication, and payment platforms vetted for security.
  • Sub-contractors who have signed confidentiality and data protection clauses.
  • Data may be transferred outside your country, but always under equivalent protection safeguards.

6. Your rights & contact details

You can request access, updates, or deletion of your data at any time. We respond within 30 days.

  • Request a copy of the data we hold on you.
  • Correct inaccurate information.
  • Withdraw consent or object to certain processing.
  • Complain to your local authority if you feel your rights were infringed.
